🚨 CE QUI MANQUE POUR SONNER VRAIMENT AMÉRICAIN
1️⃣ Il manque un “Safety Level”

Chaque slang doit avoir un indicateur :

🟢 Safe (universel, très courant)
🟡 Casual (amis / jeunes)
🔴 Risky (régional / trop internet / peut paraître immature)

Exemple :

“It’s fire” → 🟢 Safe (casual environments)

“No cap” → 🟡 Casual (jeunes surtout)

“Bussin” → 🔴 Risky (très Gen Z / peut sonner forcé)

Ça évite que l’utilisateur parle bizarrement.

2️⃣ Il manque “Generation Tag”

Gen Z
Millennial
All Ages

Parce que “lit” ≠ “fire” ≠ “awesome”

3️⃣ Il manque “Professional Alternative”

Pour chaque slang, ajoute :

Slang:
“It’s fire”

Professional version:
“That’s impressive.”
“That’s excellent.”

Ça entraîne la flexibilité.

4️⃣ Il manque “Regional Awareness”

US slang varie :

NY vibe
Cali vibe
Southern vibe

Exemple :

“Y’all” → Sud
“Deadass” → NY

Ça donne culture.

5️⃣ Il manque “Overuse Warning”

Beaucoup d’apprenants surutilisent.

Ajoute :

⚠ Don’t say “fire” 5 times in one conversation.

🔥 VERSION PREMIUM AMÉLIORÉE
It’s fire 🔥

Meaning
Something is amazing, impressive, high quality.

Vibe
Excited, energetic

Safety Level
🟢 Safe (casual settings)

Generation
All ages (more common under 35)

Region
Nationwide

Professional Alternative
“That’s impressive.”
“That’s excellent.”

Example
“Yo, your new sneakers are fire!”

Overuse Warning
Don’t use it in formal emails or meetings.

🚀 ULTRA IMPORTANT (Game Changer)
1️⃣ Add “Response Drill”

After each slang:

👉 Create 3 situations.
User must respond naturally.

Example:

Situation 1: Your friend shows you a new car.
You say: _______

Situation 2: Someone sends you a beat.
You say: _______

Ça transforme en pratique active.

2️⃣ Add “Sound Natural Score”

Quand l’utilisateur écrit :

“That concert was fire and fire and fire.”

Coach:
Naturalness score: 4/10
Overused slang detected.

3️⃣ Add “Replace Basic English”

Module spécial :

Basic → Natural Upgrade

Very good → Fire
Very expensive → Crazy expensive
Very tired → Exhausted / Dead tired

Ça accélère la fluidité.
